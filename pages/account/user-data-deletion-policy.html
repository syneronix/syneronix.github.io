CryptoBook User Data Deletion Policy

Last Updated: 19.11.2025

Welcome to CryptoBook! This document provides clear instructions on how users can fully delete their data associated with the CryptoBook App.

The CryptoBook App is designed for data privacy. We do not store any user-synced data on our own servers. All synchronized user data (files) are stored exclusively within your personal Google Drive account. The App only accesses your Google Drive and your email address for synchronization purposes.

Complete Data Deletion Instructions

To completely delete your data associated with the CryptoBook App and ensure all data is removed from your device and Google Drive, you must follow these three steps in order:

Step 1: Uninstall the App and Clear Local Data

This step ensures that all local data, cache, and settings stored on your device are permanently removed.

  1. On your device, find the CryptoBook App.
  2. Clear the App's local cache and storage (This is usually done in the device's main Settings > Apps > CryptoBook > Storage menu).
  3. Uninstall the CryptoBook App from your device.

Step 2: Revoke App Access from Your Google Account

Revoking access prevents the App from accessing your Google Drive and email address in the future.

  1. Go to your Google Account Security Settings for connected applications: Third-party apps with account access (https://myaccount.google.com/permissions).
  2. Find and click on CryptoBook in the list of applications.
  3. Click the "Remove Access" button.
  4. Confirm the action in the pop-up window.

Step 3: Delete Synced Files from Google Drive

If you have synchronized data using the App, it is stored as files or folders on your Google Drive. You must delete these files manually:

  1. Go to Google Drive: https://drive.google.com.
  2. Locate the files/folder associated with CryptoBook. (Look for a folder or files explicitly created by the App).
  3. Select all files and folders you wish to delete.
  4. Right-click and choose "Remove" (Move to Trash).
  5. For permanent deletion, navigate to the "Trash" section, select the files, and choose "Delete forever".

Note: If the App uses Google's hidden application data space (App Data Folder), this data is automatically and permanently deleted when you remove the App's access in Step 2.

Conclusion and Contact Information

Upon completion of all the steps above, all your data associated with the CryptoBook App will be deleted from your device and removed from Google Drive.

If you have questions or require confirmation regarding the removal of application access and data, you may send a request to our support team.
